‘Adventure in LALALAND’ is a commercial website with movie theme, focuses on storytelling visualization and human-computer interaction.

In this website, users will have a taste of the movie within the time of a song and explore all the possibilities by themselves. With the music, the virtual effect and the user interaction, users could have a deeper understanding towards the story itself and also the message that the movie want to tell us.

In the technical part, this project uses pure CSS, JavaScript and jQuery with no other libraries. This project gave me the opportunity to utilize all aspects of the user-centered design process, including UX Research (research, wireframing, prototyping), Design (visual, UI, interaction, motion) as well as Front-End Development (HTML/CSS/JS).

INNOVATION AWARD 2019
Role:
UX designer/ Product manager.
Project Aspects:
Research, brainstorming, ideation, design, prototyping and project management.
Timeline:
April 2019



STEM UP:
Supporting girls gain more confidence in STEAM field through hands-on experiments embedded in daily life.

Problem:
STEAM education is originated in the US and has been spread to China in recent years. But most primary school students in China receive STEAM-related education at school in the form of formal courses or at after-school educational institutions. As everyday life is another source of learning STEAM knowledge, parents have little time or attention to guide girls to explore STEAM knowledge in family life.

Goal:
Everyday life is an important source for primary school students to learn about scientific knowledge. By using the app, girls can conduct STEAM learning activities using materials in everyday life.

Design Rationale:
The app can offer simple experiments/activities based on everyday life, which can let girls learn STEAM knowledge in the real world (e.g. how to wash the dishes effectively).
The app creates gamified experience of building the child’s own house, which can motivate children to keep learning through the activities.

User & Market analysis
Through our previous interview, we found that about STEAM subjects, parents of primary school students rely much on the school education. In China, we found there is a lack of learning and guidance on STEAM in the family context. Most STEAM learning activities become a part of formal courses or courses at after-school educational institutions.Therefore, our target user is the primary school girls in China. The learning environment is their household setting.
